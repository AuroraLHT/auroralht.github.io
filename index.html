Research Experience

Oct 2018 – Present Research Assistant

Ichiro Takeuchi Research Group

University of Maryland College Park

  • Developed CRYSPNET, a neural network that generates physically meaningful crystal structures from chemical compositions, with a latent space serving as a robust materials descriptor.
  • Collaborated with NIST researchers to develop a generative adversarial network that significantly improved structure-classification accuracy for neutron diffraction data.
  • Built a U-Net–based model to segment key regions in RHEED images, enabling automated quantitative phase mapping and forming the basis for ongoing autonomous experimentation.
  • Developed a Bayesian optimization framework for direct energy deposition and demonstrated the workflow on MAR-M247.
  • Built an end-to-end image-analysis pipeline to support autonomous pulsed-laser deposition experiments, including a user-friendly interface for seamless handoff to colleagues.
  • Developed an agentic RAG system to assist with scientific research questions — the system autonomously navigates peer-reviewed literature using tools from multiple providers, synthesizes findings across papers, and draws evidence-based conclusions.
